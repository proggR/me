Exclaim! is a magazine that is by far the most authoritative source for emerging Canadian music. Operating for more than 23 years and being born from a real world community of musicians and indie radio broadcasters, Exclaim! has been the epitome of a successful startup since before startups were cool... the first time.   • Startup
  • Music
  • Community
  • Non-Profit

sourced.fm is an experiment in social-capitalism and an attempt to reimagine an industry centered around its makers and supporters. More importantly than that, its is an attempt to help emerging musicians spread beyond the borders of their city and find their audience through a supportive, regionalized community. sourced.fm is also a soon-to-be non-profit to ensure the platform remains committed to that mission above all else.
Entrada @ Queen's University: 2011-2013
  • Academia
  • Education
  • Health Services
  • Open Source
  • Developer
Entrada is an open source learning platform that emphasizes social interactions amongst students and faculty. It has strong support for accreditation objective mapping & tracking and for organizationally aware reporting. I managed installations and feedback for three of the schools using our software within the Faculty of Health Sciences, and modelled and developed features and updates with the rest of the Entrada team to meet the needs of all of the schools using and contributing to it.
Ministry of Health: 2010-2011
  • Government
  • Health Services
  • Developer
At the Ministry of Health, I developed a proof of concept web service that mediated between health care providers and the Ministry's mainframe. The REST API would store submissions in a message queue so they could be transferred to the mainframe and be processed in batch. I also wrote scripts to help automate aspects of our inventory management and had intended to develop a simple inventory management system from them before that project got denied (Advice for intrapreneurs, don't pitch the job killing app to the person who's job you're killing).
